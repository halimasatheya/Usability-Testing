Focus Groups

What is a Focus Group?

A Focus Group is a discussion that takes place with a group of users. The discussion will either be on a new proposed product or a existing product/service. The main aim of a focus group is to gather the intended users personal views, attitudes and opinions on a subject. A focus group is conducted by a moderator who asks questions or talks about a specific topic in which a small group of individuals usually around 6-8 will have a discussion. A focus group is conducted in a well known comfortable setting.

Main reasons why a Focus Group
should be conducted

Allows you to gain what the user exactly wants, by making sure the users input is implemented into the development stage means more chances of meeting the users needs and providing a more useable product. As there's no point creating an application or software in which the users needs are not even considered. Focus groups give you useful data and allow you to gain users ideas and reactions to a product. It is inexpensive so any business should be able to implement it within their product life cycle.

I conducted a focus group for a cake delivery app, a total of 6 particpants were used for the focus group. 3 females and 3 males.
Below I have included a transcript of each group and the suggestions given by users.

Group 2

Facilitator: What are your ideas on a potential cake delivery app?

Oliver: How far you deliver that kind of stuff
Mo: You can have like different categories like you can pick the base of the cake, then the toppings then you can have
Oliver: Flavour, size
Mo: Personalised message on it
Mustakim: Colours, decorations, sprinkles and whatever
Oliver: Have drop down boxes, that kind of thing select your options,

From the first focus group they wanted an interface that included drop down boxes and a similar idea to Nike id where you can preview your product so in this case a cake preview. The second focus group wished to have a create your own cake option as well as being able to see where the cake business delivers to, maps was also another element mentioned by the boys group. By conducting this focus group I myself didn't think of the ideas that the users came up with such as cake customisation on which you could make your own cake and be able to see the changes, as well as certain features as mentioned above.

Main Benefits of Focus Groups

  • Easy and quick to conduct
  • Doesn't cost much all you need is participants who will use the product
  • Doesn't require a huge amount of time
  • Allows users to define their needs
  • It isn't time constraining
  • Able to gain qualitative research
