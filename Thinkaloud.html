Think Aloud

What is Concurrent Think Aloud?

User expresses thier thoughts out aloud whilst conducting tasks

A method used to gather users opinions whilst conducting tasks users are asked to talk out aloud and say what they are thinking and what they are doing and how they feel as they carry out the task. This method gains qualitative data and allows observers to see how users complete tasks with their products/services.
However I personally believe that a think aloud method may be useful and beneficial but is it really true does the user really say what they think they may modify thier thoughts or is it a matter of the user inventing thoughts they did not have just because the observer is present and as they are being watched. Alternatively the best ways in my opinion to get true answers are to conduct a Retrospective think aloud (RTA) instead of a concurrent think aloud (CTA).

Retrospective think aloud is a method in which users carry out tasks and the task is recorded and the user sits with the facilitator and goes through their experiences once the tasks have been completed. This is less biased compared Concurrent Think Aloud.

Benefits of Retrospective Think Aloud

  • Less difficult for users to process their thoughts after the task rather than working through the task
  • Task can be conducted quickly compared to a concurrent think aloud which takes more time as user has to process their thoughts whilst completing tasks
  • Participants can perform better on tasks as they have less workload vs. concurrent think aloud in which users have double workload
  • It has a low risk of introducing fabrications
  • It isn't time constraining
  • Participant can execute a task at their own pace and are not rushed in a way that can affect their normal software usage
