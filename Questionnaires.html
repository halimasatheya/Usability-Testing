Questionnaires

What is a Questionnaire?

A questionnaire is a quick and simple tool that can be used as part of usability testing. It allows you to gain qualitative data in the form of users opinions.

A questionnaire is commonly used after a usability test has been conducted. A questionnaire can be developed specifically for your own system or you have the choice to use industry standards such as the SUS or PSUQ. Questionnaires allow you to measure factors like efficiency, users satisfaction, helpfulness.
Whether your launching a new product or redesigning its always beneficial to gain real feedback from representative users and find out what works and what doesn't. It's important to ask the right questions as you want results you can rely on.

Below are some ideas on the type of questions you can possibly ask your users to gain information:
  • Is the user able to find the information they are seeking
  • Ask the user how satisfied they are with the site or service
  • Any similar or previous experiences the user has had with the site they are viewing
  • Any issues or anything that frustrated the user using the site
  • Would the user recommend the product/service
  • Any dislikes or likes regarding the site
  • Any suggestions or improvements to the site

The example questions below can be open ended for the user to answer or a Likert scale can be used ranging from Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree

Example Questions

    Have you visited this site before?
    Could you find what you were looking for?
    Was it easy to get to the home page from the page you started on?
    Was there something missing you were expecting to see?
    Easy to read (both font style and size)?
    How did you find the layout of the site?
    If you could change one thing what would it be?

By asking the user information on important topics it allows you to gain the users views and any problems they face when using your product or service.
