Prototyping

What is Prototyping?

A draft working product which is used to gain user feedback before the final product is actually created.

A prototype is a sample of a product not the final design but what may lead up to the final design. Prototypes are used to allow designers to get feedback from proposed users. Prototyping ensures that your software meets its purpose and not just how the developer thinks it ought to be. It allows you to quickly demonstrate a product/system or a part of it to provide an overview of the system. Overall prototyping is a method that can be used to gain feedback and explore ideas with an end user or client and making sure it meets your business needs and fulfills its purpose.

Benefits of Prototyping

  • Allows you to identify and address problems in early stages of development
  • Prototyping ensures involving users actively in the development stages
  • End users receive a better understanding of the system being established
  • Can detect errors much earlier in the process
  • Gain quick user feedback which means better solutions for users
  • Functionality that isn't available can easily be identified
  • Anything confusing or difficult for end user can be identified
  • Saves time and money from having to recreate a finished product
